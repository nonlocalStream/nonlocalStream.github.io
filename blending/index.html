Part1: Hybrid Images

Favorite Result:

Me and my doggie
The original images and their FFT images
The filtered images and their FFT images

Explanation:

I apply a low-pass filter to one image and a high-pass filter. The low-pass filter is implemented by a gaussian filter. The high-pass filter is obtained by substracting the low-passed image from the original image. As we can see from the FFT image, picture1 only contains the low frequencies after being filtered and picture2 contains less low frequencies after being filter. Put two components together, we get a hybrid image that looks like my selfie when watched in distance and my dog when watched nearby.

Multiresolution Blending

Favourite result:

This is a blending on multiple resolution scales. As the following figures show, I get the laplacian stack of both images and the gaussian stack of the mask. On each level, we use the corresponding mask to blend the two corresponding images on laplacian stack. In the end, we add these blending on multiresolutions to get the final results. The gussian stack of the mask ensures that the extent transition is best suitable for the frequencies at that level.
